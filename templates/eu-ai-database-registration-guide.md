# EU AI Database Registration Guide (Article 49)

**Version:** 2.1.0
**Regulatory Basis:** EU AI Act Articles 49, 71

The EU AI Act requires that high-risk AI systems and certain other AI systems be registered in the **EU AI Database** before they are placed on the market or put into service. This guide provides a step-by-step procedure for completing the registration.

---

## 1. Who Must Register?

| Role | Registration Obligation | Legal Basis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Providers** of high-risk AI systems | Must register the system in the EU database **before** placing it on the market or putting it into service. | Article 49(1) |
| **Deployers** of high-risk AI systems | Must register their use of the system in the EU database **before** putting it into service. Deployers that are public authorities must also register the FRIA results (see [FRIA Template](fria-template.md)). | Article 49(3) |
| **Providers** of AI systems under Article 50 (transparency obligations) | Must register in the EU database before placing on the market. Applies to chatbots, deepfake generators, and emotion recognition systems. | Article 49(2) |
| **Authorised Representatives** | If the provider is established outside the EU, their EU-based Authorised Representative (Article 22) must register on their behalf. | Article 49(1) |

**Exemption:** AI systems used exclusively for military, defence, or national security purposes are exempt.

---

## 2. The EU AI Database

*   **URL:** The database is maintained by the European Commission and accessible at [https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/database/](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/database/) (public search interface) and through the official EU registration portal for submissions.
*   **Public vs. Non-Public Sections:** Most registration data is publicly accessible to promote transparency. However, certain fields may be restricted for law enforcement AI systems (Article 49(4)).

---

## 3. Required Information for Registration

The following information must be provided during registration (based on Annex VIII):

### A. Provider Information
*   [ ] Legal name, address, and contact details of the provider.
*   [ ] Legal name, address, and contact details of the Authorised Representative (if applicable).
*   [ ] Trade name or brand under which the AI system is marketed.

### B. AI System Information
*   [ ] Name and version of the AI system.
*   [ ] Description of the intended purpose of the AI system.
*   [ ] EU AI Act risk classification and the specific Annex III category (if high-risk).
*   [ ] The Member State(s) where the system is or will be placed on the market or put into service.
*   [ ] Status of the system (on the market, withdrawn, recalled).

### C. Conformity Assessment
*   [ ] Type of conformity assessment performed: **Internal Control** (Annex VI) or **Third-Party Assessment with Notified Body** (Annex VII). See the [Conformity Assessment Guide](conformity-assessment-guide.md).
*   [ ] Reference number of the EU Declaration of Conformity.
*   [ ] Name and identification number of the Notified Body (if applicable).
*   [ ] URL or reference to the CE marking documentation.

### D. Deployer Registration (if applicable)
*   [ ] Legal name and contact details of the deployer.
*   [ ] Description of the specific use and context of deployment.
*   [ ] Summary of the [Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment](fria-template.md) results (mandatory for public-sector deployers).

## 4. Registration Procedure

### Step 1: Prepare Documentation
Ensure the following are complete **before** starting the registration:
*   Technical documentation (Annex IV).
*   EU Declaration of Conformity.
*   Conformity assessment report (Annex VI or VII).
*   FRIA results (if deployer is a public authority).

### Step 2: Access the Portal
*   Navigate to the official EU AI Database submission portal.
*   Create an institutional account with the provider/deployer's legal details.
*   Designate a registration contact person.

### Step 3: Complete the Registration Form
*   Fill in all required fields per Annex VIII (see Section 3 above).
*   Upload supporting documentation where required.

### Step 4: Submit and Obtain Registration ID
*   Submit the registration.
*   The system generates a unique **EU AI Database Registration ID** — this must be referenced in all marketing materials, documentation, and the EU Declaration of Conformity.

### Step 5: Maintain and Update
*   The registration must be **kept up-to-date**. Any significant change to the AI system (e.g., change of intended purpose, update affecting risk classification) must be reflected in the database **without undue delay** (Article 49(5)).
*   If the system is withdrawn from the market, update the status accordingly.

---

## 5. Timeline

| Obligation | Deadline |
| :--- | :--- |
| Art. 50 transparency systems (chatbots, deepfakes) | **2 November 2026** |
| High-risk AI systems under Annex III | **2 December 2027** |
| High-risk AI in regulated products (Art. 6.1) | **2 August 2028** |

---

## 6. Penalties for Non-Registration

Failure to register a high-risk AI system before placing it on the market constitutes a violation of Article 49 and is subject to fines of up to **€15 million or 3% of global annual turnover**, whichever is higher (Article 99(4)).
