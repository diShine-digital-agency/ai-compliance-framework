# EU AI Database Registration

Article 49 of the EU AI Act requires the registration of high-risk AI systems in a publicly accessible **EU AI Database** before they are placed on the market or put into service. This registration obligation takes effect on **2 August 2026**.

## Who Must Register

### Providers
Providers of high-risk AI systems must register the system in the EU AI Database **before** placing it on the market or putting it into service.

### Deployers
Deployers of high-risk AI systems that are public authorities, or are bodies acting on behalf of public authorities, or are private entities providing public services, must register their use of the system.

## What Information Is Required

Annex VIII of the AI Act specifies the information to be registered:

### Provider Registration (Section A)

| Information | Description |
|---|---|
| Provider identity | Name, address, contact details |
| Authorised representative | If the provider is established outside the EU |
| Trade name | AI system trade name and additional identifiers |
| System description | General description of the AI system |
| Intended purpose | Description of the intended purpose |
| Status | Whether the system is on the market, in service, recalled, or withdrawn |
| AI Act classification | Reference to the applicable Annex III category |
| Conformity assessment | Type of procedure performed (internal control or Notified Body) |
| Notified Body | Identity (if applicable) |
| Member States | List of Member States where the system is placed on the market or in service |
| Certificate | Conformity assessment certificate (if applicable) |
| EU Declaration of Conformity | URL or reference |
| URL | Link to system information page |

### Deployer Registration (Section B)

| Information | Description |
|---|---|
| Deployer identity | Name, address, contact details |
| Provider identity | Name and contact of the AI system provider |
| System identification | Trade name and system reference |
| Intended purpose | Specific purpose for which the deployer uses the system |
| FRIA summary | Summary of the Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (Article 27) |
| Member State | Where the system is deployed |

## Registration Procedure

1. **Create an account** on the EU AI Database platform
2. **Complete the registration form** with all required Annex VIII information
3. **Submit** the registration — the system is assigned a unique EU-wide registration number
4. **Maintain** the registration — update it within 30 days of any material change
5. **Public access** — most registration information is publicly accessible (certain law enforcement and migration information may be restricted)

## Penalties for Non-Registration

Failure to register a high-risk AI system carries penalties under Tier 3:
- Up to **€7.5 million or 1% of global annual turnover**, whichever is higher

## Timeline

| Date | Obligation |
|---|---|
| **2 August 2026** | Registration required for new high-risk AI systems |
| **2 August 2027** | Registration required for legacy systems in regulated products (Annex I) |

## Relationship to Other Obligations

Registration in the EU AI Database is one step in a broader compliance workflow:

1. [Conformity Assessment](Conformity-Assessment) — completed before registration
2. EU Declaration of Conformity — drawn up before registration
3. CE Marking — affixed before market placement
4. **EU AI Database Registration** — completed before market placement or deployment
5. [Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment](Fundamental-Rights-Impact-Assessment) — FRIA summary included in deployer registration
6. Post-market monitoring — ongoing after registration
