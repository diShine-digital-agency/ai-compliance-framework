# Glossary

Key terms used in EU AI regulation and this framework, with definitions sourced from the EU AI Act, GDPR, and related legislation.

---

### A

**AI Literacy**
The skills, knowledge, and understanding that allow providers, deployers, and affected persons to make an informed deployment and use of AI systems, taking into account their rights and obligations in connection with the AI Act. Article 4 imposes a horizontal literacy obligation. See [AI Literacy Obligation](AI-Literacy-Obligation).

**AI Office (European)**
The body within the European Commission responsible for overseeing GPAI model enforcement, coordinating cross-border enforcement, issuing guidelines, and managing the EU AI Database. Operational since August 2025.

**AI System**
A machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy, that may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment and that, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers, from the input it receives, how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments (Article 3(1) of the AI Act).

**AIMS (Artificial Intelligence Management System)**
A set of interrelated or interacting elements of an organisation to establish policies and objectives related to AI, and processes to achieve those objectives. ISO/IEC 42001:2023 specifies the requirements for an AIMS. See [ISO 42001 and AI Governance Standards](ISO-42001-and-AI-Governance-Standards).

**Algorithmic Bias**
Systematic and unfair outcomes produced by an AI system for certain groups, typically defined by protected characteristics. See [Algorithmic Bias and Fairness](Algorithmic-Bias-and-Fairness).

**Adversarial Testing / Red-Teaming**
Structured testing of an AI system's resilience against adversarial inputs, prompt injection, and other attack vectors. Required for GPAI models with systemic risk under Article 55. See [General Purpose AI (GPAI)](General-Purpose-AI-GPAI).

**Annex III**
The annex to the EU AI Act listing the categories of high-risk AI systems by use case (biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement, migration, justice). See [High-Risk AI Systems](High-Risk-AI-Systems).

**Authorised Representative**
A natural or legal person located in the EU mandated by a provider established outside the EU to act on its behalf in relation to the AI Act obligations (Article 3(5)). See [EU AI Act Roles](EU-AI-Act-Roles).

### B

**Biometric Categorisation**
The use of biometric data to assign natural persons to specific categories based on personal characteristics. Categorisation based on sensitive attributes (race, religion, sexual orientation, etc.) is prohibited under Article 5(1)(g).

**Biometric Data**
Personal data resulting from specific technical processing relating to the physical, physiological, or behavioural characteristics of a natural person, such as facial images or fingerprint data (GDPR Article 4(14)).

### C

**CE Marking**
The marking affixed to a product indicating that it conforms to the applicable EU requirements. For high-risk AI systems, CE marking confirms compliance with the AI Act. See [Conformity Assessment](Conformity-Assessment).

**Conformity Assessment**
The process of demonstrating that a high-risk AI system meets the requirements of the AI Act. Two pathways exist: internal control (Annex VI) and third-party assessment by a Notified Body (Annex VII). See [Conformity Assessment](Conformity-Assessment).

**CRA (Cyber Resilience Act)**
Regulation (EU) 2024/2847 setting cybersecurity requirements for products with digital elements. See [EU Regulatory Landscape for AI](EU-Regulatory-Landscape-for-AI).

### D

**Deepfake**
AI-generated or manipulated image, audio, or video content that resembles existing persons, objects, places, or events and would falsely appear to a person to be authentic or truthful (Article 3(60)). Subject to Article 50 transparency obligations. See [Transparency Obligations (Article 50)](Transparency-Obligations).

**Data Controller**
The natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data (GDPR Article 4(7)).

**Data Processor**
A natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes personal data on behalf of the controller (GDPR Article 4(8)).

**Data Processing Agreement (DPA)**
A contract between a data controller and a data processor required by GDPR Article 28, specifying the conditions for personal data processing. See [AI Vendor Due Diligence](AI-Vendor-Due-Diligence).

**Deployer**
Any natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body using an AI system under its authority, except where the system is used in the course of a personal, non-professional activity (Article 3(4)). See [EU AI Act Roles](EU-AI-Act-Roles).

**Downstream Provider**
A provider of an AI system that integrates or builds upon a GPAI model. The downstream provider bears the obligations applicable to the resulting AI system based on its risk classification. See [General Purpose AI (GPAI)](General-Purpose-AI-GPAI) and [EU AI Act Roles](EU-AI-Act-Roles).

**DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act)**
Regulation (EU) 2022/2554 addressing ICT risk management and operational resilience in the financial sector. See [EU Regulatory Landscape for AI](EU-Regulatory-Landscape-for-AI) and [Sector Guide: Financial Services](Sector-Guide-Financial-Services).

**DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)**
A structured assessment required by GDPR Article 35 when processing is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. See [DPIA for AI](DPIA-for-AI).

### E

**EU AI Board (European Artificial Intelligence Board)**
A body composed of representatives from each Member State, ensuring harmonised application of the AI Act across the EU.

**EU AI Database**
A publicly accessible database where high-risk AI systems must be registered under Article 49. See [EU AI Database Registration](EU-AI-Database-Registration).

### F

**Foundation Model**
A large AI model trained on broad data at scale that can be adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks. The EU AI Act uses the term "general-purpose AI model" (GPAI model) for the regulatory framework. See [General Purpose AI (GPAI)](General-Purpose-AI-GPAI).

**FRIA (Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment)**
An assessment required by EU AI Act Article 27 for deployers of certain high-risk AI systems, evaluating the system's impact on fundamental rights enshrined in the EU Charter. See [Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment](Fundamental-Rights-Impact-Assessment).

### G

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**
Regulation (EU) 2016/679 governing the processing of personal data in the EU. See [GDPR and AI](GDPR-and-AI).

**GPAI (General-Purpose AI)**
An AI model trained with a large amount of data using self-supervision at scale, displaying significant generality, and capable of competently performing a wide range of distinct tasks (Article 3(63)). See [General Purpose AI (GPAI)](General-Purpose-AI-GPAI).

**GPAI Code of Practice**
A voluntary code published by the European Commission (final version July 2025) providing a framework for demonstrating compliance with GPAI obligations under Articles 53 and 55. Adherence provides a rebuttable presumption of compliance.

### H

**Harmonised Standard**
A European standard adopted by CEN/CENELEC at the request of the European Commission. Conformity with harmonised standards creates a presumption of conformity with the corresponding requirements of the AI Act (Article 40). See [Harmonised Standards and the CEN/CENELEC Standardisation Process](Harmonised-Standards).

**High-Risk AI System**
An AI system classified as high-risk under Article 6, either as a safety component of a regulated product (Annex I) or as a standalone system in a sensitive use-case category (Annex III). See [High-Risk AI Systems](High-Risk-AI-Systems).

**Human Oversight**
Measures designed to allow natural persons to effectively oversee the operation of a high-risk AI system, including the ability to understand, interpret, override, or interrupt the system (Article 14).

### I

**Importer**
A natural or legal person located in the EU that places on the market an AI system bearing the name or trademark of a provider established outside the EU (Article 3(6)). See [EU AI Act Roles](EU-AI-Act-Roles).

### M

**Model Drift**
A gradual change in an AI model's performance or behaviour over time, typically caused by changes in the distribution of input data (data drift) or changes in the underlying relationships the model learned (concept drift). A key concern for post-market monitoring. See [Post-Market Monitoring](Post-Market-Monitoring).

**Market Surveillance Authority**
A national authority designated by each Member State to enforce the AI Act at the national level. See [National Implementation by Member States](National-Implementation).

**MDR (Medical Devices Regulation)**
Regulation (EU) 2017/745 governing medical devices. AI systems that qualify as medical devices are automatically high-risk under the AI Act. See [Sector Guide: Healthcare](Sector-Guide-Healthcare).

### N

**NIS2 (Network and Information Security Directive)**
Directive (EU) 2022/2555 setting cybersecurity obligations for essential and important entities in critical sectors. See [EU Regulatory Landscape for AI](EU-Regulatory-Landscape-for-AI).

**Notified Body**
An independent conformity assessment body accredited by a national notifying authority to evaluate high-risk AI systems under Annex VII. See [Conformity Assessment](Conformity-Assessment).

### P

**PLD (Product Liability Directive)**
Directive 2024/2853 (revised) establishing strict liability for defective products, explicitly covering software and AI systems. See [AI Liability in the EU](AI-Liability-in-the-EU).

**Post-Market Monitoring**
Ongoing monitoring by providers of AI systems after market placement to ensure continued compliance and to identify and address any risks that emerge (Article 72). See [Post-Market Monitoring](Post-Market-Monitoring).

**Prior Consultation**
A procedure under GDPR Article 36 requiring the data controller to consult the supervisory authority before processing when a DPIA indicates that the processing would result in a high risk that cannot be mitigated. See [DPIA for AI](DPIA-for-AI).

**Provider**
A natural or legal person that develops an AI system or has it developed on its behalf and places it on the market or puts it into service under its own name or trademark (Article 3(3)). See [EU AI Act Roles](EU-AI-Act-Roles).

### R

**Quality Management System (QMS)**
A structured system of processes, procedures, and responsibilities required of providers of high-risk AI systems under Article 17. The QMS must cover risk management, post-market monitoring, data governance, record-keeping, and corrective actions. See [Conformity Assessment](Conformity-Assessment) and [High-Risk AI Systems](High-Risk-AI-Systems).

**Real-Time Remote Biometric Identification (RRBIS)**
The use of an AI system for biometric identification of natural persons at a distance, in real-time, in publicly accessible spaces. Generally prohibited under Article 5(1)(h), with narrow exceptions for law enforcement. See [Prohibited AI Practices](Prohibited-AI-Practices).

**Regulatory Sandbox**
A controlled environment established by national competent authorities for the testing of innovative AI systems before market deployment, under regulatory supervision (Article 57). See [AI Regulatory Sandboxes](Regulatory-Sandboxes).

### S

**Serious Incident**
An incident or malfunctioning of a high-risk AI system that directly or indirectly leads to death, serious damage to health, property, or the environment, or a serious and irreversible disruption of critical infrastructure. Must be reported within 15 days under Article 73. See [AI Incident Response](AI-Incident-Response).

**Shadow AI**
The use of AI tools by employees without formal approval, oversight, or governance by the organisation. See [Shadow AI: Risks and Mitigation](Shadow-AI).

**Substantial Modification**
A change to an AI system after it has been placed on the market or put into service that is not foreseen by the provider in the original conformity assessment and that may affect the system's compliance with the AI Act requirements. A substantial modification triggers a new conformity assessment (Article 43(4)). See [Post-Market Monitoring](Post-Market-Monitoring) and [Conformity Assessment](Conformity-Assessment).

**Systemic Risk**
A risk specific to high-impact capabilities of GPAI models, which may have a significant effect on the Union market due to reach or actual or reasonably foreseeable negative effects on public health, safety, public security, fundamental rights, or the society as a whole. Models exceeding the 10²⁵ FLOP training compute threshold are presumed to pose systemic risk. See [General Purpose AI (GPAI)](General-Purpose-AI-GPAI).

### T

**Text and Data Mining (TDM)**
The automated extraction of information from text and data for the purpose of analysis. Under the EU AI Act, providers of GPAI models must respect copyright holders' opt-outs from TDM (Article 53, referencing Directive 2019/790). See [General Purpose AI (GPAI)](General-Purpose-AI-GPAI) and [IP Protection and AI](IP-Protection-and-AI).

**Trade Secret**
Information that (a) is secret, (b) has commercial value because it is secret, and (c) has been subject to reasonable steps to keep it secret (EU Trade Secrets Directive 2016/943, Article 2(1)). See [IP Protection and AI](IP-Protection-and-AI).

### W

**Watermarking**
A technique for embedding imperceptible signals in AI-generated content (images, audio, video, text) that can be detected by automated tools to verify the content's origin. One of the technical mechanisms for implementing Article 50 transparency obligations. See [Transparency Obligations (Article 50)](Transparency-Obligations).
