# EU AI Act Penalties and Fines

The EU AI Act establishes one of the strictest penalty regimes in EU regulatory history. Article 99 sets out a three-tier fine structure, with penalties calibrated to the severity of the violation.

## Penalty Tiers

| Tier | Violation Type | Maximum Fine |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| **1** | Prohibited AI practices (Article 5) | **€35 million** or **7% of total worldwide annual turnover**, whichever is higher |
| **2** | Non-compliance with high-risk requirements, GPAI obligations, or other substantive obligations | **€15 million** or **3% of total worldwide annual turnover**, whichever is higher |
| **3** | Supplying incorrect, incomplete, or misleading information to regulators or Notified Bodies | **€7.5 million** or **1% of total worldwide annual turnover**, whichever is higher |

### Comparison with Other EU Regulations

| Regulation | Maximum Fine |
|------------|-------------|
| **EU AI Act** (Tier 1) | €35M / 7% turnover |
| **GDPR** | €20M / 4% turnover |
| **DORA** | €10M / 5% turnover (credit institutions) |
| **NIS2** | €10M / 2% turnover (essential entities) |

The AI Act's maximum penalties exceed those of the GDPR, reflecting the EU's assessment of the potential severity of AI-related harms.

## How Fines Are Calculated

When determining the amount of a fine, national authorities and the European Commission consider:

1. **Nature, gravity, and duration** of the infringement
2. **Intentional or negligent** character of the violation
3. **Actions taken to mitigate** the harm caused
4. **Degree of responsibility** — including technical and organisational measures implemented
5. **Any relevant previous infringements** by the same operator
6. **Degree of cooperation** with the supervisory authority
7. **Size and market share** of the entity committing the infringement
8. **Any other aggravating or mitigating factors**, including financial benefits gained or losses avoided

## SME and Startup Provisions

The AI Act includes proportionality safeguards for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), including startups:

- Fines are capped proportionally to avoid disproportionate impact on economic viability.
- The Commission and Member States are required to provide guidance and support for SME compliance.
- Regulatory sandboxes (Article 57) offer controlled environments for testing innovative AI before full market deployment.

## Non-Financial Enforcement Measures

Penalties are not limited to fines. National market surveillance authorities can also:

- Issue **warnings** and **formal notices**
- Require **corrective actions** within a specified timeframe
- Order the **withdrawal** of an AI system from the market
- Order the **recall** of an AI system already placed on the market
- **Restrict or prohibit** the making available of an AI system on the market
- Request **information and documentation** from providers and deployers

For GPAI models, the European AI Office can:
- Request information from GPAI model providers
- Conduct evaluations of GPAI models
- Request corrective measures
- Restrict or withdraw a model from the market

## Who Enforces

| Level | Authority | Scope |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **EU** | European AI Office | GPAI model enforcement, coordination |
| **National** | Market Surveillance Authorities | High-risk systems, prohibited practices, general enforcement |
| **National** | Notifying Authorities | Conformity assessment body oversight |
| **National** | Fundamental Rights Authorities | Fundamental rights enforcement for high-risk systems |

Each Member State must designate its national authorities. See [National Implementation by Member States](National-Implementation) for the status of national designations.

## Penalty Timeline

- **2 February 2025:** Penalties for prohibited AI practices are enforceable.
- **2 August 2025:** Penalties for GPAI non-compliance activate.
- **2 August 2026** *(pre-Omnibus)* **/ 2 December 2027** *(Omnibus)***:** Full penalty regime in effect for all obligations, including high-risk system requirements. The applicable date depends on the adoption of the Digital Omnibus on AI Regulation.

## Practical Considerations

- **Extraterritorial reach:** Penalties apply to any entity — EU or non-EU — whose AI system affects persons within the EU.
- **Cumulative liability:** Non-compliance with the AI Act can also trigger liability under the [revised Product Liability Directive](AI-Liability-in-the-EU) and potential GDPR fines if personal data is involved.
- **Documentation as defence:** Maintaining comprehensive compliance documentation (risk assessments, DPIAs, FRIAs, audit trails) is a practical defence against enforcement actions.
